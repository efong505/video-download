**AI Is Replacing Jobs in Every Industry — Here’s How to Make Yours Replace-Proof (The “Senile Einstein” Story That Led to My Book)**

I never set out to write a book about artificial intelligence.

In the fall of 2025, I was simply trying to build **ChristianConservativesToday.com** — a censorship-resistant platform for faith-driven news, voter guides, prayer walls, and ministry resources. After six hard years of college earning my degrees in Information Technology **without any AI**, I sat down expecting the same grind I had always known.

Instead, I discovered something that felt like pure gold.

I started testing AI tools — Claude, GitHub Copilot, and later Grok — to help with serverless architecture, Angular front-ends, .NET back-ends, and deployment pipelines. What would have taken a solo developer one to two years was suddenly happening in **less than one month**. The productivity jump was staggering.

But very quickly I saw the other side.

I would prompt the AI for a complex piece of code and it would deliver an elaborate, beautifully structured solution. Then I’d follow up with a simple related request — and it had no idea what I was talking about. It hallucinated. It forgot context. It was brilliant one moment and completely unreliable the next.

I started calling it my **“Senile Einstein”** — genius-level intelligence trapped in a mind that couldn’t remember what it had just said five minutes earlier.

That experience became the spark for my book **The Necessary Evil: AI, Dehumanization, and the Fight for Human Dignity in a Transhumanist Age** (Second Edition, February 2026).

### AI Isn’t Coming for Jobs — It’s Already Here and Replacing Roles Across Every Industry

At first I thought this was just a tech story. I was wrong.

As I researched deeper, I realized AI is quietly reshaping **every single industry** in 2026:

- Software developers now generate up to 41% of production code with AI tools.  
- Marketing teams create entire campaigns in minutes.  
- Healthcare, manufacturing, education, customer service, finance, law, creative fields, and even skilled trades are being transformed.  
- McKinsey estimates generative AI alone could add **$4.4 trillion** annually to the global economy through productivity gains.

The message is clear: **AI is replacing tasks, roles, and in many cases entire job categories faster than most people realize.**

### The Dangerous Middle Ground: Don’t Underestimate It… But Don’t Overestimate It Either

Here’s what I learned the hard way — and what I want every reader to understand:

**Don’t underestimate AI.**  
Even someone with my education and years of experience could be outperformed by a less-experienced person who knows how to use AI effectively. The force multiplier is that powerful. If you ignore it, you risk falling behind — or being replaced.

**But don’t overestimate it either.**  
AI is not conscious. It has no soul, no conscience, and no moral compass. It hallucinates. It reflects the biases in its training data. And it cannot replace the human relationships, creativity, discernment, and dependence on God that define a meaningful life.

This tension — the incredible power **and** the very real limitations — is exactly why I titled the book **“The Necessary Evil.”**

AI has become necessary. In a competitive world, refusing to engage with it intelligently puts individuals, families, businesses, and even nations at a serious disadvantage. But it is still a tool — powerful, useful, and dangerous if left unchecked.

### How to Make Your Job Replace-Proof

After my “Senile Einstein” wake-up call, I stopped waiting to see what would happen. I did a deep dive: current uses of AI, the upsides, the downsides, the good, the bad, and the ugly. Out of that research and my own firsthand experience, **The Necessary Evil** was born.
